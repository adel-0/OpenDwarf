# Supernatural Power-Ups — the Necromancy Path (and others)

## Necromancy — the campaign-grade upgrade [wiki]
**How**: read a slab or book containing "the secrets of life and death", found
on tables in necromancer towers (amid piles of mundane books). Requires Reader
skill — which CANNOT be trained in-game; it must be taken at character creation
(Novice suffices).

**Gains**:
- No food, no drink, no sleep — ever again. (Eliminates the entire provisioning
  problem; makes the resource-free underworld survivable.)
- Immortal (no aging).
- Reanimate corpses as undead companions, repeatedly, short cooldown — a
  renewable disposable army.
- Can stack further secrets from other towers.

**Cost**: physical attributes freeze permanently. **Ordering rule: grind
strength/agility/endurance to target BEFORE reading the slab.**

**Tower assault** [wiki]: towers are stuffed with undead; resident necromancers
re-raise corpses they can see each time you fell one. Options: kill the
necromancers first, sneak to the slab (stealth approach is well-attested), or
fight in spots where corpses fall out of the necromancers' line of sight. Take
the slab/book and leave — no need to clear.

**Campaign fit**: Phase order — (1) grind body+skills mortal, (2) tower heist →
necromancy, (3) dark fortress / underworld with no supply line needed and
expendable undead screening the demon fight.

## Other curses [prior — LIVE-VERIFY before relying on]
- **Vampirism**: gained by drinking from a vampire-tainted goblet/desecration;
  no food/drink need + stat boost, but townsfolk interactions and sleep needs
  differ; socially risky (detection → mob). Inferior to necromancy for our
  purpose.
- **Were-curse**: bitten by a werecreature → monthly transformation; the
  transformation FULLY HEALS all wounds (classic exploit: regeneration), but
  you attack everything nearby while transformed, including companions, and
  townsfolk will kill you if seen. High chaos; not recommended for an
  autonomous agent.

## Artifacts & gear [prior]
- Best obtainable adventurer gear is looted, not crafted: steel from bandit
  leaders/fortress soldiers; divine metal exists in vaults (angel-guarded,
  extreme) — treat vaults as post-demon content, not a prerequisite.
