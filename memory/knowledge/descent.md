# The Descent — Reaching the Underworld (adventurer, no digging)

## Routes — there are exactly two [wiki]
1. **Initial dark fortresses** (world-map symbol Π — the *original* goblin sites,
   not satellite pits): only sites a unique demon rose from have an underworld
   opening. Their central tower is built around an **underworld spire** of slade —
   a huge open circular stairway descending 100+ z-levels through stair-mazes to
   underworld gates.
2. **Player-made fortresses** that breached the underworld in fortress mode and
   were abandoned/retired. (Strategic option: deliberately prepare one in fortress
   mode as a controlled descent route — legal play, no adventurer digging needed.)

**Dead ends — do not chase these** [wiki]: ordinary caves and caverns do NOT
connect to the underworld for adventurers; NPC dwarven fortresses do not either,
even when worldgen claims they breached. An adventurer cannot dig. Rumors of
"caves" are tier-appropriate loot/training sites, not descent routes.

## Dark fortress anatomy [wiki]
- Simple towers amid sprawling trench networks; throne room at the TOP of the
  main tower (the demon master rules there — see `demons.md`: you can fight the
  unique demon WITHOUT entering hell).
- Ground floor + 2–4+ subterranean prison/maze floors; living pits and work pits
  hold goblins, trolls, beak dogs.
- The spire descent is long stair-mazes — provision fully beforehand; expect no
  water/food once in slade.
- Performance warning: mature dark fortresses have thousands of inhabitants —
  expect lag; traveling at night while residents sleep reduces encounters.

## The underworld itself [wiki]
- A massive open slade cavern. **Bottomless glowing pits kill instantly on
  falling in** — treat every pit edge as lethal terrain; never fight adjacent to
  an edge; never path along edges.
- **Zero resources**: no water, food, plants, or loot. Carry everything in;
  necromancer adventurers (no needs — `powers.md`) trivialize this.
- Demons spawn the moment the underworld is entered/opened — first wave can be
  10–100+, then endless groups of 1–5 wander in from map edges. There is no
  "clearing" hell; the objective form must be *enter, kill one, leave*.

## Campaign implications
- World selection (NORTHSTAR §7) must ensure goblin civs with unique demon
  rulers exist → guarantees Π sites. Verify on the world map before committing
  a campaign to a world.
- "Kill a demon" has two difficulty settings: throne-room unique demon (hard) vs
  underworld expedition (extreme). Do the first before attempting the second.
